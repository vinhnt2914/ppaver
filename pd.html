Project Description

Overview

Topic

Initially, our idea was to conduct research on GUI. This was our first step in the big picture. By completing our first milestone, we have gathered enough knowledge, therefore we decided to upgrade this topic one step further. Our goal is now to build a website that generates free UI for users. This is a difficult project that requires skills in diverse areas. Acknowledged that we only focus on building the outer face of the website, while learning and improving ourselves so that in the future time, we can give the website the functionalities that we wanted.

Motivation

We are now living in a rapidly changing world. Local shopping is something that belongs to the past. A study conducted by Google has shown that 66% of shoppers prefer online shopping to find items they’re looking for, compared to 27% who prefer offline. We believe that this difference will increase in the following years. As a result, the demand for websites and applications is now surprisingly high. However, it’s not easy to make a website. U need to know how to code and have experience in User Experience. Not everyone has the time for that. Therefore, they seek solutions online. There are already multiple UI or website-making companies on the market. However, they won’t give you everything for free, there is always a limitation, most commonly known are free trials, and limits on aspects like your web bandwidth or a custom domain. That’s why this project of ours will be completely free. Not only do we provide users with solutions to their problems, but this is also a chance for us to practice and improve our skills such as coding, advertising, team working, and to boost up our Portfolio.

Landscape

In the market right now, there are already many products like us. UI-generating website isn’t something new, it has been there for a long time like Wix, Webflow, Weebly, and GoDaddy, just to name a few. However, they don’t give u everything for free. There is always some limitations to what u can do using their service. Usually, they ended up with asking u to pay. Acknowledging that problem, we made our website completely free. Users can have access to all of our functions without having to pay a single cent. This will make sure they have the best experience.

Testing

In order to be sure that our website is finished as originally intended, our team wants to check it from the details (site appearance, performance, completeness of features). Once we've completed our self-assessment of the site, we'll put it in beta for a week and email peers asking for the review.

    1. General
  • Check the environment (browser, browser correct version)
  • Check overall screen interface
  • Labels, textboxes, combos have the same length, width and spacing, no deviation
  • Labels use the same font type, font size, left alignment
  • Check spelling, structure, grammar
  • The form is logically arranged and easy to use
  • Background colors, text colors, overall link colors
  • Check the mouse pointer icon when clicking the button or clicking on the link (whether the mouse pointer appears as a hand when moving to the button or clicking on the link)
  • Check when clicking on links (access to screen corresponding to each selected item)
  • Check pagination
  • Numbering in ascending and continuous order
  • Go back to the first, last, previous, next or any 1 page
  • The pages are clear and do not lose content when going through the page
  • Test the web for the best and clear display on all browsers with different resolutions
    2. Details
  • Check completeness of features
  • Drag and drop website builder
  • eCommerce functionality
  • Domains and hosting
  • Service and security
  • AI business tools
    3. As mentioned above, we will be recruiting a group of people to test the product before it is released. This group of people will use our website to try to create other websites with different purposes (brand websites, sales websites, education websites, etc.). After using the product, they will be asked to fill out a form to answer the following questions:
  • Is the website easy to use and convenient?
  • Do you have any difficulty using the website? (Example: No desired template, not enough features, etc)
  • Do you have any further requests from our website?

After receiving feedback from users, we will change and improve the website. Once the site is at its most complete, we'll put it in beta. With the first 20 people using the site, they will be asked to test and give feedback, these users will receive a website with the full domain name as a thank you for their contributions.

Once all the above steps have been completed, we will finalize the website and put it live. Of course, in the future when the website has been put into operation, we will still receive feedback from users and continuously update the website to become the most complete.

Risks

  1. When working as a group, there are still some hurdles engendering self-deprecating and dissenting points of view.

    2. Solutions: Sometimes, our members have different viewpoints, so we have to group up to listen and consider every opinion carefully, one by one. As a result, everyone can hear others' opinions and choose the best one.

  2. When the project is gradually being completed, some team members started to think they do too much work while others did little than them. Therefore, little thought leads to conflict in the team.

    3. Solutions: We believe that the only solution to this problem is we have to make it clear who's going to do what and take responsibility in each section before starting this project. Moreover, if anyone has completed their section, they can also support other teammates if they want.

  3. Lack of liability: sometimes some individuals forget their assigned tasks because of their personal work.

    4. Solutions: Instead of reprimanding creating a tense atmosphere, remind the member tactfully. Let them know that because of their lack of accountability, the overall results of the group are significantly affected.

  4. Miss communication: Throughout the working process, some members will do their part alone without the approval of others. Therefore, the project will not be completed and the parts of the article will not connect with each other, which kills productivity. Moreover, miss communication can also lead to conflict and misunderstanding in the team.

    5. Solutions: This problem will easily be fixed with every person's responsibility, and always take time to understand what their teammates were saying, do not assume personal perception on anyone writing. In addition, encourage members to hand out the solution or suggest the part where there are problems or mistakes.
