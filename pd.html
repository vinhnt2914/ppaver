Project Description

Overview

Topic

Initially, our idea was to conduct research on GUI. This was our first step in the big picture. By completing our first milestone, we have gathered enough knowledge, therefore we decided to upgrade this topic one step further. Our goal is now to build a website that generates free UI for users. This is a difficult project that requires skills in diverse areas. Acknowledged that we only focus on building the outer face of the website, while learning and improving ourselves so that in the future time, we can give the website the functionalities that we wanted.

Motivation

We are now living in a rapidly changing world. Local shopping is something that belongs to the past. A study conducted by Google has shown that 66% of shoppers prefer online shopping to find items they’re looking for, compared to 27% who prefer offline. We believe that this difference will increase in the following years. As a result, the demand for websites and applications is now surprisingly high. However, it’s not easy to make a website. U need to know how to code and have experience in User Experience. Not everyone has the time for that. Therefore, they seek solutions online. There are already multiple UI or website-making companies on the market. However, they won’t give you everything for free, there is always a limitation, most commonly known are free trials, and limits on aspects like your web bandwidth or a custom domain. That’s why this project of ours will be completely free. Not only do we provide users with solutions to their problems, but this is also a chance for us to practice and improve our skills such as coding, advertising, team working, and to boost up our Portfolio.

Landscape

In the market right now, there are already many products like us. UI-generating website isn’t something new, it has been there for a long time like Wix, Webflow, Weebly, and GoDaddy, just to name a few. However, they don’t give u everything for free. There is always some limitations to what u can do using their service. Usually, they ended up with asking u to pay. Acknowledging that problem, we made our website completely free. Users can have access to all of our functions without having to pay a single cent. This will make sure they have the best experience.

Detailed Description

Aims

As you can see in the Topic, our group is building a website, which generates UI for users completely free. UI design plays an important role in our daily life and also in the success of a website or an application, which is a challenging process. It maximizes a website or app's responsiveness, effectiveness, and accessibility rather than just concentrating on its appearance. When users design UI by themselves, it is vital for us to always keep the user experience and user expectations from our website in their mind. The big picture of this website is to support users as a tool, not a replacement. Besides, our website is to teach the user something from us so they can understand and utilize our service better. In order to bring the best experience to the user, we have several goals that need to be achieved to fulfill the big picture. The first thing that comes to our mind is the feedback section, which is a vital function that every website or application has to have. Both positive and negative feedback is crucial because it keeps us going, and shows us what needs to be improved in order to reach a higher level. The second thing is the search bar because it is not always easy for the user to find what they are looking for quickly. Moreover, the search bar will help the user navigate around and we do not want to force them to figure out what category the function they are looking for belongs to. The search bar is also another way to display content to the user that they would not have reached on their own. Last but not least, is the “About us” section, which is an excellent and professional way to do our own storytelling. We use this section to express our emotions, feelings, stories, and uniqueness in order to build a sense of trust or gain loyalty from the customer.

If things do not go as we expected, we will go for the feedback section after the core of the website. The feedback is fast and easy to put on the website, which is crucial for us to get the response of the user. If we have enough time and resources to finish one of our goals, we would choose the “About us” section. The “About us” section is about the same as our reputation, uniqueness, and telling our journey, it also maybe turns the potential user into our client.

Testing

In order to be sure that our website is finished as originally intended, our team wants to check it from the details (site appearance, performance, completeness of features). Once we've completed our self-assessment of the site, we'll put it in beta for a week and email peers asking for the review.

    1. General

  • Check the environment (browser, browser correct version)
  • Check overall screen interface
    1. Labels, textboxes, combos have the same length, width and spacing, no deviation
    2. Labels use the same font type, font size, left alignment
    3. Check spelling, structure, grammar
    4. The form is logically arranged and easy to use
    5. Background colors, text colors, overall link colors
  • Check the mouse pointer icon when clicking the button or clicking on the link (whether the mouse pointer appears as a hand when moving to the button or clicking on the link)
  • Check when clicking on links (access to screen corresponding to each selected item)
  • Check pagination
    1. Numbering in ascending and continuous order
    2. Go back to the first, last, previous, next or any 1 page
    3. The pages are clear and do not lose content when going through the page
  • Test the web for the best and clear display on all browsers with different resolutions

    2. Details

    Check completeness of features

    1. Drag and drop website builder
    2. eCommerce functionality
    3. Domains and hosting
    4. Service and security
    5. AI business tools

    3. As mentioned above, we will be recruiting a group of people to test the product before it is released. This group of people will use our website to try to create other websites with different purposes (brand websites, sales websites, education websites, etc.). After using the product, they will be asked to fill out a form to answer the following questions:

    1. Is the website easy to use and convenient?
    2. Do you have any difficulty using the website? (Example: No desired template, not enough features, etc)
    3. Do you have any further requests from our website?

After receiving feedback from users, we will change and improve the website. Once the site is at its most complete, we'll put it in beta. With the first 20 people using the site, they will be asked to test and give feedback, these users will receive a website with the full domain name as a thank you for their contributions.

Once all the above steps have been completed, we will finalize the website and put it live. Of course, in the future when the website has been put into operation, we will still receive feedback from users and continuously update the website to become the most complete.

Risks

  1. 3rd Party Systems conflicted or error: When we working on our project, we combine the 3rd Party systems with our work sometimes it made conflict or error. Moreover, some small mistakes like that could interrupt our working process and take more time and effort to fix.

    2. Solutions: We research all the 3rd Systems that are suitable to our work, the requirement must be fully identified and the resources must be clear, precious, and updated or tested every day because when we use that app, we also learn new things so we need to have a clear understanding about that 3rd Party. Moreover, it is very important for the overall project's success.

  2. Software is difficult to learn: When working on our project, we choose Visual Studio as our coding platform, along with learning. There is a lot going on in the GUI of Visual Studio and the probability of us not understanding what this icon or button does is very likely to happen. Interfere with something we don’t understand might severely interrupt our work. The worst thing that could happen is we accidentally delete everything.

    3. Solutions: Before working with Visual Studio or any coding platform, we must read their documentation first and watch videos about how to use them. If we came across any function that we don’t understand, we must not use it until we do.

  3. Server crash: When making and hosting a website we also have to face the risk of crashing. That risk can be from multiple sources. It could be from our host provider experiencing trouble, or our domain has expired if we happen to buy one. If not, then the problems could be from us, maybe when we add more features, our code is broken or some plugins have errors. The worst of all is malicious attacks, one of the most common is DDos

    4. Solutions: When hosting our web, we need to look for a credible host provider, especially if they offer web protection. When adding plugins or extra codes, we must double-check and test-run it first.
